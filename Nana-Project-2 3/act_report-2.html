INTRODUCTION¶

This is an analysis of the tweet archives via Twitter user @dog_rates,also known as WeRateDogs. It is described as an account that rates dogs pictures with adorable and nice comments. These ratings usually have a denominator of 10, with over 4 million followers and have received worldwide attention on twitter platform. We had access to three different datasets for the analysis, which are

  1. twitter archive: The WeRateDogs Twitter archive contains basic tweet data for 5000+ tweets
  2. twitter API - tweet Json: Back to the basic-ness of Twitter archives: retweet count and favorite count are two of the notable column omissions
  3. image prediction: a table of image predictions but we made use of tweet ID, image URL, and the image number that corresponded to the most confident predictions.

Conclusions and Limitations:¶

With an initial 2356 rows and 17 columns that was obtained at the initial stage of analysis, the cleaned datasets was 2175 rows and 15 columns shows the extent of how the recorded dataset was dirty and messy. Overall 9 quality issues and 2 tidiness issues was addressed during the wrangling process and all relevant data was merged to form a master dataset called "df_twitter_archive_master.csv". Three (3) insights was obtained from the refined dataset:

a. golden retriever been the most predicted dog breed

b. iphone as the most popular tweet source amongst users for the period under review.

c. to observe pupper been the highest dog stage

Limitations encountered was not been able to have access to the original dataset used for the dog prediction from twitter, this would have given a more beauitful insight into the quality of analysis.
